1. Introduction for video

Video-project embodies the collective voice of persons of culture working in Contemporary Art, Music, Choreography, and Curating.


Project participants live and work within different economical and political systems: in the Czech Republic, Germany, Ukraine, Russia, Sweden, Belgium, and Belarus. They are now at the beginning of their art careers; they talk about the uncertainty and the fact that their near future is economically totally unknown to them. They all have in common the impossibility of making a living out of their art, they all have other activities covering their living expenses. Some of them have already adapted, some of them accept this as normal and don’t imagine any way how things could be otherwise, some of them balance at the limit of his or her emotional and physical capacities, hoping for a change.


In our conversations they criticize the mechanisms of the cultural industry, try to fit them, or, having already got inside, think about what is next and how to survive. It is a cultural precariat that rarely thinks of itself as a community because of its fragmentation and individualism, but it can unite for the protection of their economical, labour, and political rights.


Undercover Artists is a personal and partly therapeutic project as I have been living in such a complicated (dis)balance for more than 7 years now, combining programming with documentary photography and visual art. Working on the project gave a feeling of being a community arising at the crossroads of art and capital.


Project is a audio-visual compilation based on video-calls recorded via Google Hangouts / Skype during the interview with the participants of the project. The image was made by the smartphone and laptop cameras put against each other which created an endless visual looping with different types of zoom.
